[Clinically significant drug-drug and drug-food food interactions associated with calcium channel blockers] blockers]. .
Calcium channel blockers have been widely used for treatment of hypertension , angina pectoris , arrhythmia , and other heart diseases .
Nowadays , combination therapy is widely common for these diseases .
However , combination therapy is associated with increasing risks of pharmacokinetic or pharmacodynamic drug-drug drug interaction , and therefore clinicians should pay attention to prevent it .
Furthermore , in addition to drug-drug interactions , we need to be cautions for drug interaction with food and drink including health-food and supplement .
