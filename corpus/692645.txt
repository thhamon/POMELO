The role of biogenic amines in foods .
Biologically active amines are normal constituents of many foods for example cheese , plant foods and wines .
These low molecular weight organic based do not represent any hazard to individuals , unless large quantities are ingested or natural mechanism of their catabolism are inhibited .
Investigations were performed concerning the histamine and tyramine content of the Hungarian made cheese , domestic vegetables , fruits and some other foods .
In this paper we should like to draw the attention to the problems of amines from the point of view of food hygiene and food- food-drug interactions .
